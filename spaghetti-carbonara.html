Spaghetti Carbonara

Italian Pasta Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 350g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 75g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan cheese. Season with freshly ground black pepper.
  2. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente according to package instructions.
  3. Cook the pancetta: While the pasta is cooking, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta and cook until crispy, about 5-7 minutes. If using garlic, add it during the last minute of cooking and sauté until fragrant.
  4. Combine ingredients: When the pasta is done, reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta water, then drain the pasta. Immediately add the hot pasta to the skillet with the pancetta (remove from heat first). Toss quickly to coat the pasta with the rendered fat.
  5. Add the egg mixture: Working quickly, pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta, stirring constantly. The residual heat will cook the eggs, creating a creamy sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to achieve your desired consistency.
  6. Serve: Serve immediately, garnished with additional grated cheese, freshly ground black pepper, and chopped parsley.

Tips

  • Work quickly when adding the egg mixture to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
  • Traditional carbonara does not include cream. The creaminess comes from the eggs and cheese.
  • For an authentic taste, use guanciale instead of pancetta if available.
  • Reserve some pasta water to adjust the consistency of the sauce if needed.
