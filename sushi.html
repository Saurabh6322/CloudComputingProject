Sushi

Japanese Seafood Prep time: 45 mins | Cook time: 30 mins

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 8 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 1/2 lb sashimi-grade fish (salmon, tuna, etc.)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • Soy sauce, for serving
  • Wasabi, for serving
  • Pickled ginger, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the rice: Rinse the sushi rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Combine rice and water in a rice cooker or pot and cook until tender.
  2. Make sushi vinegar: In a small saucepan, combine rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Heat until sugar dissolves, then cool.
  3. Season the rice: Transfer cooked rice to a large wooden bowl. Gradually fold in the vinegar mixture while fanning the rice to cool it. Cover with a damp cloth.
  4. Prepare fillings: Slice fish into thin strips. Cut avocado and cucumber into thin slices.
  5. Roll sushi (maki): Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo rolling mat. Spread a thin layer of rice over 3/4 of the nori, leaving the top edge bare. Arrange fillings in a line across the center. Roll tightly from the bottom, sealing the edge with water.
  6. Make nigiri: Form a small ball of rice with your hands. Place a thin slice of fish on top, pressing gently.
  7. Slice and serve: Using a sharp knife, slice the rolls into 6-8 pieces. Arrange on a plate with wasabi, pickled ginger, and soy sauce.

Tips

  • Always use short-grain sushi rice for the best texture and stickiness.
  • Keep a small bowl of water nearby to wet your hands when handling rice to prevent sticking.
  • Use a very sharp knife dipped in water to cut sushi rolls cleanly.
  • For vegetarian options, substitute fish with additional vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, or mushrooms.
