Cheeseburger

Fast Food American Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 10 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio)
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 4 slices American or cheddar cheese
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • Dill pickle slices
  • Ketchup and mustard
  • Mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the patties: Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions. Form each portion into a patty about 1/2 inch thick and slightly larger than your buns (they'll shrink when cooking). Press your thumb into the center of each patty to create a small dimple (this prevents the burger from puffing up).
  2. Season: Season both sides of the patties generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the grill or pan: Preheat a grill or skillet to medium-high heat. If using a skillet, add a small amount of oil.
  4. Cook the burgers: Place the patties on the hot surface and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side for medium doneness. For well-done burgers, cook for 1-2 minutes longer.
  5. Add cheese: During the last minute of cooking, place a slice of cheese on each patty and cover with a lid to help it melt.
  6. Toast the buns: While the burgers are cooking, lightly toast the hamburger buns on the grill or in a toaster.
  7. Assemble: Spread mayonnaise on the bottom bun, add lettuce, the burger patty with melted cheese, tomato slices, onion slices, and pickle slices. Add ketchup and mustard to the top bun, then place it on top of the assembled burger.

Tips

  • For juicier burgers, avoid pressing down on the patties while cooking.
  • Let the cooked patties rest for a minute before assembling to allow juices to redistribute.
  • For a smoky flavor, add a few drops of liquid smoke to the ground beef before forming patties.
  • Try different cheese varieties like pepper jack, Swiss, or blue cheese for unique flavors.
