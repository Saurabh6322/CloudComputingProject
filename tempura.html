Tempura

Japanese Fried Prep time: 20 mins | Cook time: 15 mins

Ingredients

  • For the batter:
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1 egg, cold
    • 1 cup ice-cold water
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • For frying:
    • Vegetable oil or canola oil (enough for 2-3 inches in a pot)
  • Vegetables and seafood (choose a selection):
    • Sweet potato slices
    • Bell pepper strips
    • Eggplant slices
    • Broccoli florets
    • Mushrooms
    • Shrimp, peeled and deveined
    • White fish fillets, cut into pieces
  • For dipping sauce:
    • 1/2 cup dashi stock (or water)
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 2 tablespoons mirin
    • 1 teaspoon grated daikon radish
    • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

Instructions

  1. Prepare ingredients: Cut vegetables into thin, even slices. Pat dry all vegetables and seafood with paper towels to remove excess moisture.
  2. Make dipping sauce: Combine dashi, soy sauce, and mirin in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer, then remove from heat and cool. Serve with grated daikon and ginger.
  3. Heat oil: In a deep, heavy-bottomed pot, heat oil to 350°F (175°C). Use a cooking thermometer to maintain temperature.
  4. Prepare batter: Just before frying, place flour in a bowl. In a separate bowl, lightly beat the egg with ice-cold water. Pour the egg mixture into the flour and mix very lightly with chopsticks. The batter should be lumpy - do not overmix.
  5. Fry tempura: Dip each piece of vegetable or seafood into the batter, allowing excess to drip off. Carefully place in hot oil and fry until golden and crisp, about 1-2 minutes for vegetables and 2-3 minutes for seafood.
  6. Drain: Remove with a slotted spoon and place on a wire rack or paper towels to drain excess oil.
  7. Serve: Serve immediately with dipping sauce while still hot and crispy.

Tips

  • Keep the batter cold at all times - you can even place the bowl in another bowl filled with ice.
  • Do not overmix the batter - lumps are good and create a light, crispy texture.
  • Fry in small batches to maintain oil temperature.
  • For shrimp, make small cuts along the belly and straighten to prevent curling during frying.
  • Serve immediately after frying for the best texture and flavor.
