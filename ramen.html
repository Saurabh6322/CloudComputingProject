Ramen

Japanese Soup Prep time: 30 mins | Cook time: 3 hours

Ingredients

  • 4 packages ramen noodles (discard seasoning packets)
  • For the broth:
    • 8 cups vegetable or chicken stock
    • 1/4 cup soy sauce
    • 2 tablespoons mirin
    • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1-inch piece ginger, sliced
    • 1 onion, halved
    • 2 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • For toppings:
    • 4 soft-boiled eggs, halved
    • 1 cup bean sprouts
    • 4 green onions, sliced
    • 1/2 cup corn kernels
    • 4 sheets nori, cut into strips
    • Sliced mushrooms (shiitake or enoki)
    • Sliced protein of choice (tofu, chicken, pork)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot, combine stock, soy sauce, mirin, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, onion, and dried mushrooms. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 2-3 hours. Strain and return to pot.
  2. Prepare toppings: Soft-boil eggs (6-7 minutes), then place in ice water. Peel and set aside. Prepare other toppings as needed.
  3. Cook noodles: In a separate pot, cook ramen noodles according to package instructions, usually 2-3 minutes. Drain and rinse with cold water to stop cooking.
  4. Assemble bowls: Place cooked noodles in bowls. Ladle hot broth over noodles.
  5. Add toppings: Arrange toppings on top of the noodles - half an egg, bean sprouts, green onions, corn, nori strips, mushrooms, and protein.
  6. Serve: Serve immediately while hot. Provide additional toppings on the side for customization.

Tips

  • For a richer broth, simmer for longer (up to 6 hours).
  • Make the broth a day ahead for deeper flavor.
  • For a vegetarian version, use vegetable stock and mushrooms for umami flavor.
  • For a spicy version, add chili oil or sriracha to taste.
  • Store components separately if making ahead - broth, noodles, and toppings should be combined just before serving.
