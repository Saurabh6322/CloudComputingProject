French Fries

Fast Food Side Dish Prep time: 20 mins | Cook time: 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 2 quarts (2 liters) vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • Optional seasonings: garlic powder, paprika, or herbs

Instructions

  1. Prepare the potatoes: Wash and peel the potatoes. Cut them into 1/4-inch thick sticks. Try to keep them uniform in size for even cooking.
  2. Soak the potatoes: Place the cut potatoes in a large bowl of cold water and let them soak for at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight in the refrigerator). This removes excess starch for crispier fries.
  3. Dry thoroughly: Drain the potatoes and pat them completely dry with paper towels. Any moisture will cause the oil to splatter.
  4. Heat the oil: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or deep fryer, heat oil to 325°F (165°C). Use a thermometer for accuracy.
  5. First fry (blanching): Working in batches, fry the potatoes for 3-4 minutes until they're soft but not browned. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Let them cool completely (about 30 minutes).
  6. Second fry (crisping): Increase the oil temperature to 375°F (190°C). Fry the blanched potatoes again in batches for 3-4 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Season: Transfer to a paper towel-lined bowl, sprinkle with salt and any other desired seasonings while still hot. Toss gently to coat evenly.
  8. Serve: Serve immediately while hot and crispy with your favorite dipping sauces.

Tips

  • The double-frying method is key to achieving crispy exteriors and fluffy interiors.
  • Russet potatoes work best for fries due to their high starch content.
  • For healthier fries, you can bake them at 425°F (220°C) for 25-30 minutes after tossing with a little oil.
  • Try serving with different dipping sauces like garlic aioli, spicy ketchup, or truffle mayo.
