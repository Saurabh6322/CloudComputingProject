Hyderabadi Biryani

Indian Main Course Prep time: 1 hour | Cook time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • For the meat:
  • 1 kg meat (chicken or mutton), cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons chopped mint leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • Salt to taste
  • For the rice:
  • 3 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 6 cups water
  • 5-6 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • For layering:
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced and fried until golden
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • 1/4 cup milk mixed with a pinch of saffron
  • 2 tablespoons rose water (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons kewra water (optional)
  • Fresh mint and coriander leaves

Instructions

  1. Marinate the meat: In a large bowl, mix all the ingredients for the meat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  2. Prepare the rice: Bring water to a boil in a large pot. Add all the whole spices and salt. Add the soaked and drained rice. Cook until the rice is 70% done (about 5-7 minutes). Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the marinated meat: In a heavy-bottomed pot, add the marinated meat and cook on medium heat until it's about 80% done, about 15-20 minutes for chicken or 30-40 minutes for mutton.
  4. Layer the biryani: In the same pot with the meat, layer half of the partially cooked rice. Sprinkle half of the fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves. Drizzle half of the saffron milk, rose water, and kewra water.
  5. Add the final layer: Add the remaining rice, fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves. Drizzle the remaining saffron milk, rose water, and kewra water. Pour ghee around the edges.
  6. Seal and cook: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal it with dough or a heavy weight to prevent steam from escaping. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes.
  7. Rest and serve: Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid. Gently mix the layers before serving.

Tips

  • Use good quality, aged basmati rice for the best results.
  • The key to a good biryani is the layering and the dum cooking (slow cooking in sealed pot).
  • You can add potatoes to the meat layer for an authentic Hyderabadi touch.
  • Serve with raita (yogurt dip) and mirchi ka salan (green chili curry).
