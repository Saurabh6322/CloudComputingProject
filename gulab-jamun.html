Gulab Jamun

Indian Dessert Prep time: 20 mins | Cook time: 30 mins

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk (as needed)
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For sugar syrup:
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon rose water
  • Few strands of saffron (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Prepare the sugar syrup: In a wide pan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened. Add cardamom pods, rose water, saffron (if using), and lemon juice. Keep the syrup warm.
  2. Make the dough: In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Mix well.
  3. Add fat: Add ghee to the dry ingredients and rub it in until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  4. Form the dough: Gradually add milk, a little at a time, and knead to form a soft, smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  5. Shape the jamuns: Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls. Ensure there are no cracks on the surface.
  6. Heat the oil: Heat oil for deep frying on medium-low heat. The oil should not be too hot.
  7. Fry the jamuns: Gently slide the dough balls into the hot oil and fry on low to medium heat, stirring occasionally, until they turn golden brown.
  8. Soak in syrup: Immediately transfer the fried jamuns into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours before serving.
  9. Serve: Serve warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped pistachios if desired.

Tips

  • The dough should be soft but not sticky. If it's too dry, add a little more milk; if too sticky, add a bit more milk powder.
  • Fry the jamuns on low to medium heat to ensure they cook evenly from inside out.
  • The jamuns will double in size when soaked in the syrup, so make them small.
  • Adding a little lemon juice to the syrup prevents crystallization.
