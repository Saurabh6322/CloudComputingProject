Butter Chicken

Indian Main Course Prep time: 30 mins | Cook time: 45 mins

Ingredients

  • 800g boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons tandoori masala
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1 tablespoon dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, mix chicken pieces with tandoori masala, 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Cook the marinated chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a pan and cook the marinated chicken until it's about 80% done. Remove and set aside.
  3. Prepare the gravy: In the same pan, add the remaining oil and butter. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add spices: Add the remaining ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add tomato puree: Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture, about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Add cream: Lower the heat and add the heavy cream. Stir well to combine.
  7. Add chicken: Add the partially cooked chicken pieces to the gravy and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and the gravy thickens.
  8. Final touches: Crush the dried fenugreek leaves between your palms and sprinkle over the curry. Adjust salt to taste.
  9. Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.

Tips

  • For a richer flavor, use bone-in chicken pieces and remove the bones before serving.
  • If the gravy is too thick, add a little water or chicken stock to adjust the consistency.
  • The fenugreek leaves add an authentic flavor to butter chicken, try not to skip this ingredient.
