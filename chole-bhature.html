Chole Bhature

Indian Street Food Prep time: 8 hours | Cook time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • For Chole (Chickpea Curry):
  • 2 cups chickpeas (kabuli chana), soaked overnight
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2 teaspoons chole masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 tea bag (for color)
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • For Bhature:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Warm water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the chickpeas: Drain the soaked chickpeas and pressure cook them with salt, bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, and a tea bag until soft (about 15-20 minutes). Remove the tea bag and set aside.
  2. Make the chole base: Heat oil in a large pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add spices: Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for 2 minutes. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and chole masala. Mix well.
  4. Add tomatoes: Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Add chickpeas: Add the cooked chickpeas along with some of the cooking water. Mash a few chickpeas to thicken the gravy. Simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Finish the chole: Add garam masala and adjust salt. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  7. Prepare bhature dough: In a large bowl, mix flour, semolina, yogurt, sugar, baking soda, oil, and salt. Knead into a soft dough using warm water as needed. Cover and let it rest for at least 2-3 hours.
  8. Roll the bhature: Divide the dough into small portions. Roll each portion into an oval or round shape.
  9. Fry the bhature: Heat oil for deep frying. Slide the rolled bhature into hot oil and press gently with a slotted spoon until it puffs up. Flip and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  10. Serve: Serve hot chole with freshly fried bhature, along with sliced onions and lemon wedges.

Tips

  • The tea bag adds a dark color to the chole, which is characteristic of Delhi-style chole.
  • For softer bhature, add a tablespoon of semolina to the dough.
  • The longer you let the bhature dough rest, the softer and fluffier they will be.
