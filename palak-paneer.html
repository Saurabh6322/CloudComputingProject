Palak Paneer

Indian Vegetarian Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 30 mins

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 500g spinach (palak), washed thoroughly
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi)

Instructions

  1. Blanch the spinach: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add spinach and blanch for 2-3 minutes. Drain and transfer to ice water to stop the cooking process.
  2. Blend the spinach: Drain the cooled spinach and blend it with green chilies to make a smooth puree. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the paneer: Lightly fry the paneer cubes in a little oil until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  4. Make the base: Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add spices: Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add tomato puree: Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate, about 3-4 minutes.
  7. Add spinach puree: Add the spinach puree and mix well. Cook for 5-7 minutes on medium heat.
  8. Add paneer: Add the fried paneer cubes and garam masala. Mix gently and simmer for 5 minutes.
  9. Finish the dish: Crush the dried fenugreek leaves between your palms and sprinkle over the curry. Add fresh cream and mix gently.
  10. Serve: Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Tips

  • Blanching the spinach helps retain its vibrant green color.
  • For a richer version, add an extra tablespoon of cream or a knob of butter at the end.
  • You can make your own paneer at home by curdling milk with lemon juice and pressing the curds.
