Grilled Salmon

Healthy Seafood Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 12 mins

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each), skin on
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the marinade: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dill, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate the salmon: Place the salmon fillets in a shallow dish, skin side down. Pour the marinade over the salmon, making sure to coat all sides. Cover and refrigerate for 15-30 minutes (not longer, as the acid in the lemon juice will start to "cook" the fish).
  3. Preheat the grill: Heat your grill to medium-high heat (about 375-400°F). Clean and oil the grill grates to prevent sticking.
  4. Grill the salmon: Place the salmon on the grill, skin side down. Close the lid and cook for 6-8 minutes without moving it. This allows the skin to crisp and the fish to release naturally from the grates.
  5. Flip and finish cooking: Carefully flip the salmon using a wide spatula. Cook for another 2-4 minutes, or until the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork. The internal temperature should reach 145°F.
  6. Serve: Transfer the salmon to plates and serve immediately with lemon wedges. Garnish with additional fresh dill if desired.

Tips

  • If you don't have a grill, you can use a grill pan on the stove or bake the salmon in the oven at 400°F for 12-15 minutes.
  • Look for wild-caught salmon when possible for the best flavor and nutritional profile.
  • Don't overcook salmon - it continues to cook slightly after being removed from the heat.
  • For a cedar plank salmon variation, soak a cedar plank in water for at least 1 hour, then place the salmon on the plank to grill for a smoky flavor.
