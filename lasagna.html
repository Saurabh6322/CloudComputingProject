Lasagna

Italian Pasta Prep time: 45 mins | Cook time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • For the meat sauce:
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 pound Italian sausage, casings removed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (28 oz each) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cans (6 oz each) tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp dried basil
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the cheese mixture:
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 4 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup Parmesan cheese, grated

Instructions

  1. Prepare the noodles: Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and lay them flat on parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  2. Make the meat sauce: In a large pot, brown the ground beef and Italian sausage over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and cook until softened. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, sugar, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Prepare the cheese mixture: In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Preheat the oven: Set your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Assemble the lasagna: In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread 1 cup of meat sauce on the bottom. Arrange 3 lasagna noodles over the sauce. Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by 1 cup of meat sauce, and 1 cup of mozzarella cheese. Repeat the layers twice more.
  6. Add the final layer: Top with the remaining 3 noodles, the remaining meat sauce, the remaining mozzarella cheese, and all of the Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake: Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  8. Rest and serve: Let the lasagna rest for 15 minutes before cutting and serving. This allows the layers to set and makes it easier to serve.

Tips

  • You can make the meat sauce a day ahead to enhance the flavors.
  • No-boil lasagna noodles can be used to save time.
  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the meat with layers of sautéed vegetables like zucchini, eggplant, and bell peppers.
  • Lasagna freezes well. Wrap individual portions for quick future meals.
