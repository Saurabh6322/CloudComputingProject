Rogan Josh

Indian Main Course Prep time: 30 mins | Cook time: 1.5 hours

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb (preferably shoulder or leg), cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 tablespoons Kashmiri red chili powder (for color and mild heat)
  • 1 teaspoon regular red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground fennel seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 cup yogurt, whisked
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the lamb: Wash and pat dry the lamb pieces. Set aside.
  2. Heat the ghee: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the ghee or oil over medium heat.
  3. Add whole spices: Add bay leaves, cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add onions: Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown, about 8-10 minutes.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste: Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add lamb: Add the lamb pieces and sear on all sides until they change color, about 5-7 minutes.
  7. Add ground spices: Add Kashmiri red chili powder, regular red chili powder, ground cumin, coriander, fennel, and ginger. Mix well to coat the meat.
  8. Add yogurt: Lower the heat and slowly add the whisked yogurt, stirring continuously to prevent curdling.
  9. Simmer: Add water and salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 1-1.5 hours or until the meat is tender and the gravy has thickened.
  10. Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.

Tips

  • For an authentic Kashmiri flavor, use Kashmiri red chili powder which gives a vibrant color without excessive heat.
  • You can use a pressure cooker to reduce cooking time to about 30 minutes.
  • The dish tastes even better the next day as the flavors have time to meld together.
