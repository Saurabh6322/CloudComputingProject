Fried Chicken

Fast Food Southern Prep time: 30 mins | Cook time: 20 mins

Ingredients

  • 8 pieces chicken (mix of thighs, drumsticks, and breasts)
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Vegetable oil for frying (about 3-4 cups)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, combine buttermilk and hot sauce. Add chicken pieces, making sure they're fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  2. Prepare the coating: In a large shallow dish, combine flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper. Mix well.
  3. Heat the oil: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or deep skillet, heat oil to 350°F (175°C). Use a thermometer to maintain consistent temperature.
  4. Coat the chicken: Remove chicken from buttermilk, allowing excess to drip off. Dredge each piece thoroughly in the flour mixture, pressing to adhere. Shake off excess.
  5. Fry the chicken: Carefully place a few pieces of chicken in the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd. Fry for 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden brown and internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  6. Drain and rest: Transfer fried chicken to a wire rack set over a baking sheet or to paper towels to drain excess oil. Let rest for 5-10 minutes before serving.
  7. Serve: Enjoy hot with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or biscuits.

Tips

  • For extra crispy chicken, double-dip by dredging in flour, then quickly in buttermilk, then in flour again.
  • Maintain oil temperature between 325-350°F (165-175°C) for perfect frying.
  • If chicken is browning too quickly, reduce heat slightly.
  • For a healthier version, you can bake the coated chicken at 400°F (200°C) for 40-45 minutes.
